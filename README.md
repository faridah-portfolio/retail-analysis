## 📌 Project Overview

This end-to-end Power BI project analyzes retail transaction data across five product categories — Beauty, Clothing, Electronics, Food, and Sports — to help business stakeholders make faster, evidence-backed decisions on inventory planning, marketing spend, and customer targeting.

The dashboard consolidates 1,000 transactions into a single-page, fully interactive report with real-time slicer filtering by age group and product category.

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### KPI Cards
Four headline cards — Total Revenue, Unique Customers, Transaction Count, and Avg Order Value — give immediate business context before any deeper exploration.

### Category Revenue Bar Chart
Horizontal bar chart ranking all five product categories by total revenue, making the performance gap between Electronics and other categories immediately visible.

### Gender Split Donut Chart
Visualises the Male/Female revenue distribution (49.64% vs 50.36%), confirming broad demographic reach with no dominant skew.

### Monthly Revenue Table
A matrix table breaking down revenue by category across every month, enabling granular month-over-month and category-level comparisons.

### Monthly Trend Line Chart
Tracks total revenue across the full year to surface seasonality patterns and inform campaign timing.

### Total Amount by Month & Category (100% Stacked Bar)
Shows each category's proportional contribution to monthly revenue — useful for spotting shifts in category dominance over time.

### Age vs. Spend Scatter Plot
Maps the relationship between customer age and total spend, colour-coded by product category, to reveal demographic spending patterns.

### Customer Detail Table
Transaction-level drill-down showing Customer ID, Total Amount, Quantity, Product Category, and Gender for granular customer analysis.

### Interactive Slicers
- **Age Group** — filter all visuals by customer age bracket (18–25, 26–35, 36–45, 46–55, 56–69)
- **Product Category** — isolate any combination of the five categories across the entire report

## 💡 Key Insights

- **Electronics dominates revenue** — it accounts for the largest category share by far, driven by a high average order value rather than transaction volume alone.
- **Gender distribution is nearly equal** (49.64% Female / 50.36% Male) — the customer base is demographically balanced, with no single segment requiring outsized prioritisation.
- **Food has the highest transaction frequency but the lowest avg order value** — a clear signal for bundling and cross-sell strategies to increase basket size.
- **Revenue shows a downward trend toward February** — indicating potential seasonality that could be countered with targeted Q1 promotions.
- **A small number of customers account for disproportionate spend** — the customer table reveals high-value individuals worth loyalty investment.

---

## ✅ Business Recommendations

1. **Double down on Electronics marketing** — it already leads revenue; promotional campaigns during peak months could compound that advantage significantly.
2. **Introduce Food bundles** — pairing low-AOV Food items with complementary categories (Beauty, Sports) can lift basket value without heavy discounting.
3. **Launch a Q1 retention campaign** — the revenue dip toward February suggests post-holiday disengagement; a targeted offer in January could reverse the trend.
4. **Build an age-based segmentation strategy** — the scatter plot reveals distinct spending patterns by age group; personalised communication will outperform blanket campaigns.
5. **Reward high-value customers** — Customer ID 1004 (Electronics, $349.31) and similar profiles represent a VIP tier worth protecting with a loyalty programme.
